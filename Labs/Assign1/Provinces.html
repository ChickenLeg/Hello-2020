Alberta

Alberta is bordered by the provinces of British Columbia to the west and Saskatchewan to the east, the Northwest Territories to the north, and the U.S. state of Montana to the south.

British Columbia

It is the westernmost province of Canada, located between the Pacific Ocean and the Rocky Mountains. With an estimated population of 5.1 million as of 2019, it is Canada's third-most populous province.

Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan is a landlocked province with large distances to moderating bodies of waters. As a result, its climate is extremely continental, rendering severe winters throughout the province.

Manitoba

It is often considered one of the three prairie provinces (with Alberta and Saskatchewan) and is Canada's fifth-most populous province with its estimated 1.369 million people.

New Brunswick

It is one of four Atlantic provinces on the east coast of Canada. According to the Constitution of Canada, New Brunswick is the only bilingual province.

Newfoundland and Labrador

It is composed of the insular region of Newfoundland and the continental region of Labrador to the northwest, with a combined area of 405,212 square kilometres (156,500 sq mi).

Nova Scotia

It is the country's second most-densely populated province and second-smallest province by area, both after neighbouring Prince Edward Island.

Ontario

It is Canada's most populous province, with 38.3 percent of the country's population, and is the second-largest province in total area, It is home to the nation's capital city, Ottawa.

Quebec

It is the only one to have a predominantly French-speaking population, with French as the sole provincial official language.

Prince Edward Island

It is the smallest province of Canada in both land area and population, but it is the most densely populated. Part of the traditional lands of the Mi'kmaq.
